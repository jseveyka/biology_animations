Ocean Acidification and Calcification

A simplified, interactive model of how atmospheric carbon dioxide changes seawater chemistry and shell-building.

When more CO2 dissolves in seawater, more H+ forms. H+ reacts with carbonate, reducing CO32− availability. With less carbonate available, organisms have a harder time forming CaCO3 shells and skeletons. This model shows that CO2 does not directly dissolve shells here; it lowers carbonate availability. Bicarbonate can still matter biologically, but this pathway depends on available CO32−.

Key Ideas

  • Binary fission produces two genetically similar daughter cells from one parent cell.
  • In ideal conditions, each generation doubles the population.
  • This produces exponential growth.
  • A log-transformed graph makes exponential growth easier to recognize because it becomes a straight line.
  • During plateau/stationary phase, growth rate equals death rate; the population size stays about the same even though cells are still dividing and dying.
  • After the plateau phase, a death phase can occur if conditions worsen.

Calculation Help

Formula: Original # of bacteria/mL = colony count ÷ (dilution × plated volume in mL)

Colony count comes from the selected countable plate.

Dilution is the tube label, such as 10-5.

Plated volume is the volume chosen above.

Key Biology Ideas

Serial dilutionEach transfer reduces the bacterial concentration by a known factor.
Visible coloniesEach colony is assumed to grow from one viable bacterium or a small group of bacteria.
Too many coloniesDense lawns and overlapping colonies cannot be accurately counted.
Too few coloniesSmall counts have higher sampling error because chance matters more.
Countable platesMicrobiology labs often use plates with 25–250 colonies for estimates.

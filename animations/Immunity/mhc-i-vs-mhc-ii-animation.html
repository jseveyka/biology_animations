MHC I vs. MHC II Antigen Presentation

Follow the origin of the antigen: outside the cell for MHC II, inside the cell for MHC I.

MHC I:

  • Found on most nucleated body cells
  • Presents proteins made inside the cell
  • Displays many samples of internal proteins
  • Important for detecting virus-infected or abnormal cells
  • Recognized by CD8 cytotoxic T cells

MHC II:

  • Found on antigen-presenting cells
  • Presents material taken in from outside the cell
  • Uses phagocytosis, phagosome formation, and phagolysosome digestion
  • Important for activating helper T cells
  • Recognized by CD4 helper T cells

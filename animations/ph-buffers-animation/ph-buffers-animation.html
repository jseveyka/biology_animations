Key ideas

  • pH measures how acidic or basic a solution is.
  • More H⁺ means lower pH.
  • More OH⁻ means higher pH.
  • Pure water has small amounts of H⁺ and OH⁻.
  • Buffers resist changes in pH by binding or releasing H⁺.
  • A buffer does not stop pH change completely; it reduces the size of the change.

HA is a weak acid: it can donate H+.

A− is the conjugate base: it can accept H+.

Together they form a buffer pair that resists changes in pH.

Acid added: A− accepts H+ and becomes HA.

Base added: HA donates H+ and becomes A−.

Unbuffered Acid causes a large pH drop; base causes a large pH rise.
Buffered Acid and base cause smaller pH changes.

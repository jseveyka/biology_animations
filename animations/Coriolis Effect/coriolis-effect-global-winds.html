Coriolis Effect and Global Surface Winds

Global circulation cells

Global circulation cells

Three looping circulation cells form in each hemisphere. Air rises near the equator, sinks near 30°, rises again near 60°, and sinks near the poles.

Surface winds begin because air moves from high pressure toward low pressure. Earth’s rotation then deflects that moving air.

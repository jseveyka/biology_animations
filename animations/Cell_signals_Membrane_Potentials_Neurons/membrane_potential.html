Membrane Potential

Intracellular voltage: 0 mV relative to extracellular fluid

This simplified model shows membrane voltage as a charge difference across the membrane. When positive ions leave the intracellular fluid, the inside becomes more negative relative to the outside. When positive ions move back in, the voltage moves back toward zero or becomes more positive.

This is a conceptual model. Real resting membrane potential also depends on ion concentration gradients, selective membrane permeability, and ion pumps.

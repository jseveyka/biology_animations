Student explanation

Cells can increase exchange with the environment by increasing surface area without increasing volume. Flattening a cell or folding the membrane increases surface area, while the amount of cytoplasm can stay the same. This increases the surface-area-to-volume ratio, which can improve diffusion and transport.

Folding is shown as a simplified 2D representation: a smooth membrane boundary gains perimeter while its enclosed area is rescaled to stay constant, representing increased 3D membrane surface area.

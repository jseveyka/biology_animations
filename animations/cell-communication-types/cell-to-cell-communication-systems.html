Gap junctions

What is happening?

Gap junctions are direct cytoplasmic connections between neighboring cells. Small ions and molecules can move from one cell to another without entering the extracellular fluid.

Watch for

Ions pass through one connecting channel, staying inside the two neighboring cells.

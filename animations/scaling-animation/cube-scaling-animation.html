Key scaling ideas

  • As side length increases, volume grows faster than surface area.
  • A cube twice as long has 4 times the surface area and 8 times the volume.
  • The surface-area-to-volume ratio decreases as the cube gets larger.
  • This is why small cells or organisms have proportionally more surface area compared with their volume.

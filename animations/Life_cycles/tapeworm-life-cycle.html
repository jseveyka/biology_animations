1. Eggs on vegetation

Step 1 of 12

Eggs in feces may contaminate nearby vegetation, soil, or water.

Tapeworm eggs are the environmental stage that can be swallowed by an intermediate host.

Notes

  • The pig or cow is the intermediate host.
  • The human is the definitive host because the adult tapeworm reproduces in the human intestine.
  • The cysticercus is the larval stage in muscle.
  • Proglottids are tapeworm body segments.
  • Gravid proglottids contain eggs.
  • Undercooked infected pork or beef can transmit cysticerci to humans.

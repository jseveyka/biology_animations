Counter-Current Heat Exchange in the Testes

Arterial blood leaves the body cavity at 37°C and cools as heat transfers to adjacent venous blood. Venous blood returning from the testis gains heat and approaches body temperature as it nears the body cavity.
This helps keep the testis cooler than body temperature for normal sperm production.

Counter-current exchange cools arterial blood before it reaches the testis.

Key ideas

  • Arterial blood enters the spermatic cord from the body cavity at about 37°C.
  • Venous blood returning from the testis runs next to the artery.
  • Heat moves from the artery into the vein before arterial blood reaches the testis.
  • A relaxed scrotum increases heat loss when the testis is warm.
  • Cremaster and dartos contraction reduce heat loss when the testis is cool or sympathetic activity is high.

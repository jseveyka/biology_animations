Each gas contributes part of the total pressure. A gas with a higher concentration creates a larger partial pressure because more of those molecules are hitting the walls.

Relative solubility

  • CO₂very soluble
  • O₂moderate
  • N₂least soluble
At the same partial pressure, different gases dissolve in different amounts. Carbon dioxide dissolves much more readily than oxygen or nitrogen. Increasing gas pressure drives more gas into solution until equilibrium is reached.
Equilibrium means the gas pressure above the fluid and the gas pressure represented in the fluid are equal, but the amount dissolved depends on solubility.

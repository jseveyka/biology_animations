Educational Notes

  • Hydrostatic pressure pushes fluid.
  • Oncotic pressure pulls fluid toward proteins.
  • Capillary hydrostatic pressure is higher at the arteriole end and lower at the venule end.
  • Albumin in the capillary creates capillary oncotic pressure.
  • Positive NFP means filtration out of the capillary.
  • Negative NFP means absorption into the capillary.
  • This is a simplified classic Starling-force model for teaching.
